# EmoLab — Ethics and Limitations

## Purpose

EmoLab supports inspection of multimodal affect-related model outputs. It does not provide objective access to a person's internal emotional state.

## Model Outputs Are Predictions

Outputs may be uncertain, incomplete, inconsistent, culturally dependent, affected by recording quality, influenced by training data, or sensitive to segmentation.

## Cross-Modal Disagreement

Different modalities may produce contradictory outputs. EmoLab treats disagreement as information to inspect and does not automatically determine which modality is correct.

## Human Interpretation

EmoLab supports human review but does not replace contextual understanding, participant self-report, domain expertise, qualitative research, statistical analysis, ethical review, or clinical judgment.

## Affective-Cognitive Agent

The agent converts selected telemetry into structured hypotheses. It may omit context, inherit source-model errors, overinterpret weak signals, or generate plausible but incorrect explanations.

The agent's output is not psychological ground truth.

## Prohibited Uses

EmoLab is not intended for consequential decisions concerning diagnosis, employment, insurance, access to services, admissions, law enforcement, criminal justice, credibility, immigration, psychological profiling, security clearance, or disciplinary action.

It must not be used for covert surveillance or non-consensual emotional profiling.

## Privacy and Consent

Responsible use should consider informed consent, purpose limitation, data minimization, retention, access control, secure storage, withdrawal procedures, and legal or institutional requirements.

## Cultural and Contextual Limitations

Affective expression and interpretation are influenced by culture, language, social context, identity, neurodiversity, disability, communication style, situational factors, recording environment, and relationships.

## Facial Analysis Limitations

Facial observations may be affected by lighting, camera angle, occlusion, image quality, pose, coverings, morphology, skin tone, motion blur, multiple visible people, or tracking errors.

## Speech and Language Limitations

Speech and language outputs may be affected by transcription errors, speaker-attribution errors, accents, dialects, code-switching, noise, overlap, irony, sarcasm, indirect communication, incomplete utterances, and language coverage.

## Persistence

Preserved outputs may reflect older model versions or outdated service behaviour. Persistence improves reproducibility but does not validate stored predictions.

## Core Principle

> Model-generated affective predictions and generated interpretations should be inspectable, contextualized, auditable, and challengeable—not presented as unquestionable facts about a person.
